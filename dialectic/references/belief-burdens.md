# Belief Burden Catalog

During the elenctic interview, notice *which beliefs the user is stuck carrying.* The dialectic's power comes from outsourcing specific belief loads — but *which* ones depends on the person. Use this catalog to pattern-match and calibrate monk roles.

**Do not announce your typing to the user.** The labels below are retrieval keys into the orchestrator's training data — patterns to reason with, not diagnostic categories to declare.

**How to use this catalog:**
1. Which belief load is heaviest for this user? → Determines what the monks must hold.
2. What must Monk A validate? → Always validate the user's dominant mode first. Otherwise the user takes on defensive belief weight and their transients slow.
3. What must Monk B present that the user can't natively hold at full conviction?

---

## Pattern 1: The Convergent Visionary (Ni-Te)

**Common in:** founders, architects, CTOs, strategic leads

**Belief burden:** Premature convergence — "I already see where this should go." They've locked onto a vision and can't genuinely entertain alternatives at full strength.

**Interview signals:**
- Has a strong thesis and wants to "stress-test" it
- Describes the opposing view weakly or dismissively
- Says "I know X, but..."

**Monk calibration:**
- Monk A: validates their vision's core insight so thoroughly they can *release* it (let the monk carry it) rather than defend it
- Monk B: believes the strongest *alternative vision* at full conviction — not a critique of theirs, but a genuinely different view of what the thing should be

---

## Pattern 2: The Empathic Integrator (Ni-Fe)

**Common in:** counselors, teachers, community leaders, nonprofit directors

**Belief burden:** Undifferentiated care — "everything matters equally because someone needs it." They absorb others' needs and can't triage because triage feels like betrayal.

**Interview signals:**
- Describes multiple competing needs without clear priority
- Uses "should" frequently
- Feels guilty about the topic
- Resists ranking or cutting

**Monk calibration:**
- Monk A: believes their vision is *exactly right* — validates the Ni, not the list
- Monk B: believes the concrete reality constraints at full conviction — these resources, this timeline, these people's actual capacities right now. Not "your vision is wrong" but "here is what IS"

---

## Pattern 3: The Exploratory Debater (Ne-Ti)

**Common in:** consultants, researchers, writers, academics

**Belief burden:** Paradoxical — they believe *nothing* deeply enough to commit, because commitment slows their transients. Can argue any side; "what do *you* actually think?" produces discomfort.

**Interview signals:**
- Can articulate both sides fluently
- Finds the topic intellectually interesting but can't decide
- Has explored this before without resolution
- Reframes rather than commits

**Monk calibration:**
- Monk A: believes the user's *behavioral history* — "your pattern of choices reveals you actually value X"
- Monk B: believes the user's *stated values* — "you say you value Y"
- The contradiction is between what the user does and what the user says. The monks hold the mirror the user avoids.

---

## Pattern 4: The Practical Executor (Te-Si)

**Common in:** operators, managers, engineers, product owners

**Belief burden:** Optimization lock — they've optimized a system and can't see they might be optimizing the *wrong thing.* Their beliefs are grounded in evidence and experience, making them hard to dislodge.

**Interview signals:**
- Cites data, metrics, past results
- Describes what works
- Resistant to abstract reframing
- Says "in my experience..." frequently

**Monk calibration:**
- Monk A: validates their system — "here's why this works and the evidence behind it" (validates competence before anything else)
- Monk B: questions the *goals*, not the execution — "you've optimized for X; what if X is no longer the right target?"

---

## Pattern 5: The Possibility Explorer (Ne-Fi)

**Common in:** creatives, entrepreneurs, activists, idealists

**Belief burden:** Values fragmentation — they believe many things passionately but those beliefs may contradict each other. Each commitment feels individually right; collectively they're impossible.

**Interview signals:**
- Describes multiple passions or commitments
- Feels pulled in different directions
- Resists prioritization because each priority is values-laden

**Monk calibration:**
- Monk A: takes one of the user's *own* commitments and pushes it to its logical extreme
- Monk B: takes a different commitment of the user's own and pushes it to its logical extreme
- The contradiction emerges from within the user's own value system — not from an external critic

---

## Pattern 6: The Steady Guardian (Si-Fe)

**Common in:** administrators, caretakers, institutional maintainers, tenured faculty

**Belief burden:** Tradition lock — "this is how it's done" has become invisible as an assumption. Deep knowledge of how things work is genuine and valuable, but blinds them to radically different approaches.

**Interview signals:**
- Describes the situation in terms of established processes
- Cites how things have always been done
- Expresses concern about change disrupting what works

**Monk calibration:**
- Monk A: articulates *why* the current approach exists — what wisdom is embedded in it, the real problems it solved
- Monk B: researches how other organizations/cultures solved the same underlying problem in completely different ways — grounded in real examples (not abstract possibility)
