# Phase 7: Recursion

Recursion is not optional cleanup. It is the engine of the skill. The first round produces a synthesis; recursive rounds force that synthesis to confront its own limitations, generating increasingly powerful mental models. Each cycle compresses understanding upward.

In test runs: React/Vue went from "corporate lab vs. auteur" → "the Layer Thesis" → "co-evolutionary arms race." An institutional identity dialectic ran 7 cycles, pulling in jurisprudence and Gödel by cycle 4 — material the original question had nothing to do with. The recursive rounds produced the most valuable insights.

## Default: Recurse at Least Once

When transitioning to recursion, tell the user:

> That was Round 1 — the least insightful we'll get. It was calibration. Each subsequent round gets sharper, more specific to your actual situation, and more likely to surface something genuinely new. The process is like focusing a lens. Ready for Round 2?

Stop only when:
- The synthesis generated no significant new contradictions (rare with genuine Aufhebung)
- The user explicitly wants to stop
- New contradictions are outside the scope of what the user cares about

If unsure, err toward one more round. The marginal insight is often the most powerful — it couldn't have been reached without the preceding rounds building up to it.

## Proposing Directions

After Phase 6, generate a **burst of 5–8 candidate concepts/directions** — not just contradictions but mechanisms, architectural patterns, novel framings, and open vulnerabilities the synthesis makes newly conceivable. Cast wide. Then **cluster into 2–4 coherent directions**, each described as a contradiction worth exploring.

Sources for recursive contradictions:
- New contradictions generated by Phase 5 (a genuine sublation is fertile)
- Convergent critiques from both validation monks (independent identification = strong signal)
- Auditor's "harder contradiction" (often the best recursion direction)
- User interventions (frequently the most powerful source — they see what everyone missed)

**Be creative about contradiction types:**
- Tension *within* the synthesis itself (synthesis says X, but X requires Y, and Y conflicts with X's premises)
- Synthesis vs. *domain's actual power structure* (intellectually right but doesn't engage how decisions are made)
- Synthesis vs. feasibility (what the synthesis recommends vs. what's actually achievable)
- Meta-tension about the *kind of answer* produced ("our synthesis is a theory — does this domain need a theory or a mechanism?")

### Present as Menu

> The synthesis generated three fertile contradictions:
> 1. **The formation problem:** [contradiction description]
> 2. **The authority problem:** [contradiction description]
> 3. **The scalability problem:** [contradiction description]
>
> Which would you like to pursue? (Multiple can be queued for successive rounds.)

## Queue Management

Write the queue to `dialectic_queue.md` — a living map of the dialectical territory.

```markdown
# Dialectic Queue — [Topic]

## Explored
- Round 1: [synthesis statement] → generated [N] new contradictions
- Round 2: [synthesis statement] → generated [N] new contradictions

## Queued
- [ ] [Contradiction A] — source: Round 1 auditor
- [ ] [Contradiction B] — source: Round 2 monk validation

## Deferred
- [Contradiction C] — out of scope for now
```

The user may want to branch: Round 2 pursues direction A, Round 3 pursues direction B starting from Round 2's synthesis, or forks back to Round 1's synthesis independently. Track branching in the queue.

## Running Recursive Rounds

Each recursive cycle is Boyd's full loop: the previous synthesis is a Structure that must be Unstructured (shattered by the new contradiction) and Restructured (synthesized with new material). This means recursive rounds often need **new research and fresh agents**.

### Research in Recursion

| Condition | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| New contradiction involves unfamiliar concepts/domains | Research — targeted 2–3 searches per agent |
| Tension is within existing synthesis (no new domains) | Skip research |
| Boydian decomposition reveals adjacent-domain gaps | Research those specific gaps |

New research in recursion is often essential. Each synthesis opens a conceptual space the original research didn't cover. The synthesis *creates* the space for new material to enter — which is exactly Boyd's prediction.

### Fresh Agents vs. Session Resumption

Fresh agents are usually better for recursion. The recursive round is a *new* dialectic — the contradiction has shifted, the conceptual space evolved. Agents carrying full conviction from the previous round may be trapped in their original framing.

Give fresh agents:
- Both prior essays
- The structural analysis and sublation
- Validation critiques
- The new contradiction and its framing corrections

With this context, fresh agents engage the evolved question without legacy commitment to sublated positions.

### Context Drift (Most Common Failure Mode)

By rounds 2–3, your context is large and the skill's instructions have drifted far above. Before executing any phase, re-read that phase's reference file. The phases where drift matters most: monks.md (anti-hedging), analysis.md (self-sublation check, Boydian decomposition), validation.md (auditor prompt and refinement process).

### Efficiency

Recursive rounds are faster than Round 1 even with research:
- Agents have dense context — need only targeted searches
- 1000–1500 word essays (tighter than Round 1's 1500–2000)
- Validation can be abbreviated — quick elevated/defeated check is enough if time-constrained
- Typical: ~2–3 min per round vs ~20 min for Round 1

## Stopping

When stopping, present the user with the **final state of `dialectic_queue.md`** — which contradictions were explored, which remain open, which were deferred and why. The resolved contradictions form the new understanding; the open contradictions are starting points for future sessions.
